Project Brief

Our project aims to enhance Access Power Systems' online presence, ensuring a seamless and informative platform for current and potential customers. The focus is on improving user engagement, accessibility, and showcasing the range of solar products offered.

Timeframe

1 Month

Industry

Renewable Energy

An Introduction to the Problem

Access Power Systems existing challenge in optimizing the distribution and accessbility of the innovative solar products. On top of that, customers couldn't have access to solar products and view necessary information to enable them make a purchase. This inefficiency was at odds with Access Power Systems ambitious 10x growth goals.

Utilizing XFN Teams

We began our journey with close collaboration. Leveraging on the existing team's data, we gained access to their processes, analyzing deep into the problem's core. This aided us in setting the stage for our mission to transform the interface and experience.

To commence our course, I worked closely with the project manager and meticulously outlined each of the features to be developed and prioritized them. In this phase, we worked to determine milestones from high priority to low, shaping the blueprint for our path.

Design Discovery & collaboration

To redesign the interface into a user-friendly website, I conducted a comprehensive audit. I facilitated a “design jam” workshop with project managers, software engineers, and solar energy leads. Together, we reviewed the information collections pages for each flow for both existing and new. This collaborative session clarified our process and also highlighted design considerations.

Design Phase & Sprints

The major milestone involved redesigning the website's MVPs, requiring close collaboration with the design team to align the design and data with existing client-facing challenges and address the unique demands of the website. We embraced the agile approach, working in phases to complete each sprint. Our initial priority was the redesign of the landing page, and as we progressed through each sprint, we gained valuable insights, allowing us handle simpler pages in groups of multiples per sprint.

Some issues, such as changes in additional pages displaying more products and data + primary navigation elements, were left for future expeditions. However, we documented these as "ultimate objective" to ensure they wouldn't be missed in the future scope of our design.

Beyond the Landing Page Design

At Access Power Systems, my role in design involved creating a modern style for the UI. This involved making crucial design decisions for the brand's refined look, strategizing on how to ensure seamless alignment of the product, planning sprints and agile approach with engineering to execute future sprints in proceeses.

Conclusion

In the end, as the sole UI/UX Designer on this team, I led Access Power Systems redesign on a transformative journey. We successfully addressed the challenges, turning it into a user-friendly experience and visually pleasing interface. Doing that, laid the groundwork for Access Power Systems scalability and growth aspirations. My involvement in Access Power Systems redesigning efforts, showcased my ability to contribute to broader strategic initiatives.
